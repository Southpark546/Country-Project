Megadiversity

Mexico is one of the world's megadiverse countries, harboring between 10 and 12% of the world's species in its territory.

Species Richness

Mexico ranks second in reptile species richness, third in mammals, fourth in amphibians, and fifth in vascular plants globally.

Endemic Species

Mexico has a high number of endemic species, including 77% of cacti, 32% of mammals, and 11% of bird species.

Ecosystem Diversity

Mexico's terrestrial portion includes nearly all vegetation types described worldwide, rivaling countries like China, India, Peru, and Colombia.

Conservation Efforts

Mexico has established various conservation initiatives, including the System of Management Units for the Conservation of Wildlife (SUMA) covering 19% of the national area.

Biodiversity Monitoring

Mexico has implemented high-level biodiversity monitoring in 51 targeted subareas, including equipment and training for mammal and avian monitoring.
