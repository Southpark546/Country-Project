Pre-Columbian Era

Mesoamerica saw the rise of complex civilizations with advanced writing systems and political structures. The Aztec Empire, centered in Tenochtitlan (modern-day Mexico City), was one of the most powerful states in the Americas before European contact.

Spanish Conquest

In 1519, a small Spanish army led by Hernán Cortés landed at Veracruz, marking the start of Spain's conquest of Mexico. By 1521, allied with local anti-Aztec forces, Cortés' men captured Tenochtitlan, ending the Aztec Empire.

Colonial Period

From 1521 to 1820, Mexico formed part of the Viceroyalty of New Spain. This period saw the introduction of European culture, religion, and institutions, profoundly shaping Mexican society.

Independence

The Mexican War of Independence began in 1810 with the Grito de Dolores by Miguel Hidalgo y Costilla. After a decade of conflict, Mexico gained independence in 1821, briefly becoming an empire before transitioning to a federal republic in 1824.

Key Events in Mexican History

1519: Cortés lands in Mexico.
1521: Fall of Tenochtitlan to Spanish forces.
1810: Beginning of the Mexican War of Independence.
1821: Mexico gains independence from Spain.
1836: Texas declares independence from Mexico.
1846-1848: Mexican-American War.
1910-1920: Mexican Revolution.
1917: Current Mexican Constitution adopted.
1938: Nationalization of the oil industry.
